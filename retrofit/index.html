Postcode Summary: XXXXX

This page provides a summary of the Postcode level domestic gas and electricity consumption data published by the Department of Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ).

Historical domestic gas and electricity emissions

This chart shows the annual emissions from this postcode of gas and domestic buildings' electricity consumption. Use the drop down menu to select either total, median, or mean emissions. Note that some areas are off the gas grid, so no emissions are reported. In these places, heating may be provided by some other fuel type, such as coal, wood, or oil. Emissions have been calculated by Carbon & Place by multiplying energy consumption by annual emissions factors for the UK. For electricity consumption, emissions per kWh have declined in recent years, resulting in falling emissions even if electricity consumption has remained constant.

Chart type:

Historical gas consumption

This chart shows domestic buildings' annual gas consumption in kilowatt hours in this postcode. Use the drop down menu to select either total, median, or mean emissions. Note that some areas are off the gas grid, so no emissions are reported. In these places, heating may be provided by some other fuel type, such as coal, wood, or oil.

Chart type:

Historical electricity consumption

This chart shows the annual electricity consumption in kilowatt hours by domestic buildings in this postcode. Use the drop down menu to select either total, median, or mean emissions. Consumption estimates are provided for different meter types (all, standard, and economy 7). However, data is not always available for all meter types.

Chart type:

Historical gas and electricity meters

This chart shows this postcode's total number of domestic gas and electricity meters. Electricity meters are also broken down into standard and economy 7 metres. In theory, the total number of all electricity meters should match the total of standard and economy 7 meters. However, there can be slight differences or even missing data due to how the data is reported. Therefore, two columns are shown for electricity meters to show the different counts.

Changes in the number of meters may indicate house building/demolition and the difference between gas and electricity meters can indicate the presence of off-gas grid homes.

Neighbouhood Summary: XXXXX

This report summarises the Domestic Energy Performance Certificates from 2024. Not every home has an EPC, and some homes that no longer exist may have and EPC. If more than one EPC exists for the same dwelling only the most recent one is used.

EPC ratings

A summary of the headline EPC ratings for deomestic buildings in this LSOA. From A (most energy efficent) to G (least energy efficent).

Building type

A summar of the types of domestic buildings.

Tenure

A summary of type of tenure as recored on the EPC. Not that is may have changed after the EPC was issued. Also as EPCs are required for rental properties but only required for owner-occupited properties at the time of sale, rental propery may be over-represented in the EPC data.

Building Age

A summary of the estimated construction date of dwellings.

Floor rating

The floors' energy efficency is rated from Very Good to Very Poor. Not this classification only applies to grownd floor properties, so flats often are classified as "Dwelling Below".

Floor Description

Ground floors of homes typically come in two types. Suspended floors are raised slightly above ground level and outside air can circualte in the void benith the floor to prevent mould and damp. This was common for homes built before the 1940s. Converslty solid floors are built directly on the ground usually of concrete and are more common in post-1940s homes. Both floor types can results in heat loss if not insualted, but the way the floors are insualted is different. This chart shows the disitrbution of different floor types.

Windows rating

The windows' energy efficency is rated from Very Good to Very Poor.

Glazing type

A summary of the type of glazing used.

Hot water system rating

The energy efficency of the hot water system is rated from Very Good to Very Poor.

Hot water system description

A description of how hot water is provided. The most common approach is for hot water to be provided by the main heating system. However some homes have separte space and water heating systems.

Walls rating

The external walls' energy efficency is rated from Very Good to Very Poor.

Walls description

A dewcription of the construction of the external walls. The most common types of wall a brick or block cality walls, which are cheap and easy to insulate with cavity wall insualtion. Other types of wall require more expensive internal or external insulation.

Roof rating

The roofs energy efficency is rated from Very Good to Very Poor. Dwellings with another dwelling above, such as flats are consider to not have a roof and a recorded as "Dwelling Above" only the top floor flat would have a roof rating.
